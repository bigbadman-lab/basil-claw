Executive Summary

Introduces the Cryptoassets and Digital Finance Bill to position the UK as a global hub for cryptocurrency and blockchain innovation.

Establishes a 10% Capital Gains Tax rate on cryptoassets, subject to review after two years.

Creates a two-year Blockchain Financial Services Sandbox under the FCA.

Prohibits banks and payment providers from denying services solely due to lawful crypto involvement.

Establishes a sovereign Bitcoin Reserve Fund within the UK’s official reserves.

Requires HMRC to accept approved cryptocurrencies for tax payments.

Imposes statutory time limits on government crypto-related taskforces.

Frames the measures as enabled by post-Brexit regulatory autonomy.

Problem Definition

The paper states that 7 million people in the UK hold cryptoassets, including 1 in 4 individuals aged 18–34. It argues that existing financial services have neglected the needs of younger citizens and that regulatory complexity has limited innovation.

It contends that EU regulatory frameworks, including the Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) Regulation, constrained the UK’s ability to adopt competitive tax and regulatory policies prior to Brexit.

The Bill seeks to address perceived barriers to crypto entrepreneurship, banking access for crypto users, and the absence of state-level digital asset adoption.

Key Proposals / Recommendations
Proposal: 10% Capital Gains Tax on Cryptoassets

Objective: Create a competitive and simplified tax regime for cryptoasset gains.

Mechanism: Apply a 10% CGT rate on gains exceeding the annual exempt amount under the Taxation of Chargeable Gains Act 1992; supersedes higher rates while preserving lower rates where applicable.

Expected Impact: Increased onshoring, higher transaction volumes, improved tax compliance, and potential revenue increases.

Implementation Notes: Treasury must review the impact within two years and report to Parliament.

Proposal: Two-Year Blockchain Financial Services Sandbox

Objective: Facilitate innovation in blockchain-based financial services.

Mechanism: FCA, in consultation with the Treasury, establishes a two-year sandbox allowing temporary relief from specified financial services requirements.

Expected Impact: Accelerated development and testing of crypto and distributed ledger products.

Implementation Notes: Participants must comply with consumer protection, AML, and counter-terrorist financing rules; FCA must report findings within 90 days of sandbox conclusion.

Proposal: Banking Non-Discrimination Duty

Objective: Protect lawful crypto users from denial of financial services.

Mechanism: Prohibits financial institutions from denying or restricting services solely due to lawful crypto involvement; burden of proof rests on the institution.

Expected Impact: Reduced “de-banking” of crypto users and businesses.

Implementation Notes: FCA and PRA oversee compliance; customers may file complaints with the FCA; legitimate, objectively justified refusals remain permitted.

Proposal: Sovereign Bitcoin Reserve Fund

Objective: Diversify UK reserve assets and signal institutional adoption of digital assets.

Mechanism: Treasury establishes and manages a Bitcoin Reserve Fund as part of official reserves.

Expected Impact: Reserve diversification and potential long-term appreciation.

Implementation Notes: Quarterly reporting to Parliament; annual independent audit; Bitcoin as principal holding; other cryptoassets may be approved by the Treasury.

Proposal: Payment of Taxes in Cryptocurrency

Objective: Enable cryptoasset use in discharging tax liabilities.

Mechanism: HMRC must accept approved cryptoassets; tax value determined at receipt using HMRC-published exchange rates.

Expected Impact: Integration of crypto into public finance systems.

Implementation Notes: HMRC may convert crypto to sterling or transfer to the Bitcoin Reserve Fund; annual reporting to Parliament required.

Proposal: Rapid Policy-Making Timelines

Objective: Prevent prolonged regulatory delay in crypto policy.

Mechanism: Limits government crypto-related taskforces to 90 days, extendable by 30 days with parliamentary approval.

Expected Impact: Accelerated policy development and regulatory responsiveness.

Implementation Notes: Interim report at 45 days; transparency requirements for terms of reference and final reports.

Legal / Structural Considerations (if applicable)

Amends operation of the Taxation of Chargeable Gains Act 1992 for cryptoassets.

Establishes statutory duties for FCA, PRA, HMRC, and the Treasury.

Provides Treasury authority to disapply or modify enactments to give effect to the sandbox.

Extends to the whole of the United Kingdom.

Tax provisions commence on 6 April; other provisions on Royal Assent.

Includes severability clause preserving remaining provisions if part is invalidated.

Operational / Logistical Considerations (if applicable)

HMRC must develop systems for crypto tax payments and publish valuation mechanisms.

Treasury responsible for risk management and security of the Bitcoin Reserve Fund.

FCA required to define eligibility criteria and regulatory relief parameters for sandbox participants.

Annual and quarterly reporting obligations across multiple provisions.

Taskforces subject to statutory deadlines and parliamentary oversight for extensions.

Risks, Criticisms, or Trade-offs (if mentioned)

Treasury required to review CGT impact after two years, implying uncertainty regarding fiscal effects.

Sandbox exemptions must balance innovation with consumer protection and AML/CTF compliance.

Establishment of a Bitcoin reserve introduces asset price volatility exposure.

Implementation requires new administrative systems within HMRC and regulatory bodies.
