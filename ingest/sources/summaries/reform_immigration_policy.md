Executive Summary

Proposes a five-year emergency programme to identify, detain, and deport illegal migrants in the United Kingdom.

Estimates that over 1,000,000 people have no lawful right to remain in the UK.

Establishes a new UK Deportation Command with enhanced data-sharing and enforcement powers.

Expands detention capacity to 24,000 places and enables detention-on-arrest with no bail.

Introduces scaled-up charter deportation flights, including RAF contingency support.

Seeks new and reinforced return agreements with countries of origin, using incentives and sanctions.

Projects net fiscal savings of over £7 billion in the first five years and £42 billion over the first decade.

Problem Definition

The paper states that successive governments have failed to reduce illegal immigration over three decades. It estimates that the population of individuals with no lawful right to remain in the UK exceeds 1,000,000.

It argues that their continued presence:

Undermines the rule of law.

Imposes significant accommodation and welfare costs.

Distorts low-wage labour markets.

Signals weak border control internationally.

It also claims current policy costs approximately £7 billion per year, excluding additional pressures such as impacts on the NHS.

Key Proposals / Recommendations
Proposal: Five-Year Emergency Programme

Objective: Rapidly identify, detain, and deport illegal migrants in the UK.

Mechanism: Legislative reset combined with national-scale enforcement and foreign policy measures.

Expected Impact: Large-scale removals and deterrence of future unlawful presence.

Implementation Notes: Framed as a time-limited emergency approach.

Proposal: UK Deportation Command

Objective: Centralise and intensify enforcement operations.

Mechanism: Creation of a dedicated enforcement unit, including an Illegal Migrant Identification Centre using expanded data-sharing powers.

Expected Impact: Faster identification and detention of illegal migrants.

Implementation Notes: Automatic data-sharing between Home Office, NHS, HMRC, DVLA, banks, and police; expanded biometric capture; designed to address prior enforcement gaps.

Proposal: Secure Immigration Removal Centres (SIRC)

Objective: Increase detention capacity.

Mechanism: Construction of modular facilities in remote locations with capacity for up to 24,000 detainees within 18 months.

Expected Impact: Enables detention-on-arrest and supports deportation of up to 24,000 individuals per month.

Implementation Notes: Prefabricated two-person rooms, on-site medical suites, robust perimeter security; no bail provision.

Proposal: Initial Voluntary Return Window

Objective: Encourage self-deportation before enforcement escalation.

Mechanism: Six-month Assisted Voluntary Return period with financial incentives and supporting app.

Expected Impact: Reduction in enforcement burden prior to large-scale raids.

Implementation Notes: Precedes intensified enforcement operations.

Proposal: Scaled Deportation Flights

Objective: Ensure consistent removal capacity.

Mechanism: Up to five charter flights per day; RAF Voyager aircraft maintained in six-hour standby as contingency.

Expected Impact: Operational continuity and prevention of flight cancellations.

Implementation Notes: Legal reset intended to prevent last-minute legal interventions blocking departures.

Proposal: Return Agreements and Diplomatic Leverage

Objective: Secure cooperation from countries of origin.

Mechanism: Prioritised foreign policy engagement; consular support for identification; use of incentives and penalties.

Expected Impact: Increased acceptance of returnees.

Implementation Notes: Diplomatic triage by relationship; potential cessation of visa approvals and sanctions as leverage.

Proposal: Third Country Partnerships

Objective: Provide additional removal pathways.

Mechanism: Agreements with third countries and use of British Overseas Territories as fallback; reference to Ascension Island airfield capacity.

Expected Impact: Alternative destinations for removal if origin-country returns are constrained.

Implementation Notes: Budget allocated for negotiations.

Legal / Structural Considerations (if applicable)

Requires new legislation to enable expanded data-sharing across public bodies and private institutions.

Introduces a “legal reset” intended to limit the ability of activist lawyers to halt deportation flights.

Removes bail in favour of detention-on-arrest.

Operational / Logistical Considerations (if applicable)

18-month construction timeline for 24,000 detention spaces.

Modular, remote-site accommodation model.

Capacity planning for up to five deportation flights per day.

RAF aircraft maintained as operational contingency.

Deployment of consular staff to assist identification processes.

Allocation of budget for third-country negotiations.

Risks, Criticisms, or Trade-offs (if mentioned)

Gross implementation cost estimated at £10 billion over five years.

Large-scale detention and deportation operations imply significant administrative and logistical demands.

Reliance on foreign government cooperation for return agreements.
